2.1 Schrodinger equation in first and second quantization

States of many indistinguishable particle can be specified in terms of a basis of independent-particle states, ψ i , i = 1 , … , N , where, N is the maximum number of independent particle wave-functions which is considered for the many particle states. In principle, for N → ∞ , it is considered as a complete set of states. The basis of many particle states can be formed as product of ψ ' s with the condition that is symmetric for bosons and anti-symmetric for fermions. Hence, basis-states for fermions are built as a determinant of given sets of ψ i ' s which is called Stater-determinant which gives us the anti-symmetric wave-functions. This states can be represented as, | Ψ > = | n 1 , n 2 , … , n k , … , n N > , (2.1.1) where, n k means that n k particles is in the unperturbed single-particle energy eigenstate, ψ k and these ψ k ' s can be orthonormal sets. which gives, ∑ i n i = N p (2.1.2) where, N is the total number of particles in the system.
